NTU Racing · Sep 2021 – Jun 2022

NTU Racing Suspension-related Brackets Design

Designed and analyzed multiple suspension brackets for the NTU Racing Formula Student car, including rear a-arm brackets, tie rod brackets, rear shock absorber brackets, and brake light brackets. All components were designed to withstand high suspension forces (approx. 3300 N for rear shock absorbers) with a safety factor larger than 3, then manufactured through welding and grinding.

Key Results

  • All 4 bracket types met SF > 3 under worst-case suspension loading
  • Rear shock bracket designed for 3300 N with minimum weight penalty
  • All parts manufactured and installed successfully on competition vehicle
  • Compiled full cost reports and engineering drawings per FSAE rules

Challenges & Solutions

As a first-year team member, learning to weld and grind structural components while maintaining dimensional accuracy required close mentorship and multiple prototype iterations. The shock absorber bracket was the most challenging due to its compound angles and the need to avoid interference with suspension arm travel. This was resolved by iterating between SolidWorks motion simulation and physical mock-up fitting before committing to final welds.
