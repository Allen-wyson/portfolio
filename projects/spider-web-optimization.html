Strength-to-Weight Ratio Optimization of Spider-web Structure

Used finite element analysis and a genetic algorithm to maximize the strength-to-weight ratio of spider-web-inspired truss structures. Built parametric ANSYS FEA models and drove the optimization loop with a MATLAB genetic algorithm, varying thread radii, angles, and spiral densities to identify superior topologies for lightweight load-bearing applications.

Key Results

  • Achieved 30%+ improvement in strength-to-weight vs. baseline uniform web
  • Identified optimal radial-to-spiral thread ratio for distributed loads
  • GA converged within 50 generations across varied initial populations
  • Validated optimized topology in ANSYS under multiple load cases

Challenges & Solutions

The genetic algorithm's fitness evaluation required running a full FEA solve for each candidate, making the optimization expensive. This was mitigated by reducing mesh density during GA iterations (coarse mesh for fitness ranking) and reserving fine-mesh validation for the top-N candidates each generation. Constraint handling for minimum thread radius was implemented as a penalty term in the fitness function.
