Strength-to-Weight Ratio Optimization of Spider-web Structure

Developed a data-driven workflow to maximize the strength-to-weight ratio of bioinspired spider-web geometries. Used Latin Hypercube Sampling to explore a discrete design space (radial/spiral thread counts and radii), automated SolidWorks CAD generation via Python, ran ANSYS FEA to evaluate each candidate, then applied Bayesian Optimization (Gaussian Process surrogate + Expected Improvement acquisition) to converge on the optimal design in 20 FEA iterations.

Key Results

  • Optimal design: 5 radial & 5 spiral threads, radii 2.5 mm & 0.5 mm
  • Best strength-to-weight ratio: 13,225 N/kg
  • Bayesian Optimization converged within 20 costly FEA evaluations
  • Load capacity precision: ±0.1 MPa across optimization runs

My Contribution

  • Modeled the spider-web geometry and defined parameterized design variables (thread counts, radii)
  • Ran FEA simulations in ANSYS and evaluated performance metrics for each candidate design
  • Led the Bayesian Optimization loop and convergence testing, ensuring efficient exploration of the design space

Challenges & Solutions

Each fitness evaluation required a full FEA solve, making brute-force search infeasible across the design space. Bayesian Optimization addressed this by fitting a Gaussian Process surrogate to already-evaluated designs and using Expected Improvement to intelligently select the next experiment — balancing exploration of unseen regions with exploitation of promising ones. This reduced the required FEA calls from hundreds to just 20 while still finding the global optimum.
