EPFL · Fall 2024 · ME-425

Cruise Controller for a Car on Highway

Implemented a full Model Predictive Control (MPC) framework for autonomous highway driving in MATLAB. The controller handles both lateral (lane-keeping) and longitudinal (velocity tracking) dynamics via analytically linearized subsystems. Advanced deliverables include offset-free tracking via disturbance estimation, tube-based MPC for safe car-following with guaranteed minimum gap, and Nonlinear MPC (NMPC) for overtaking maneuvers with ellipsoidal obstacle constraints.

Key Results

  • Lane change completed in ≤ 3 s without overshoot
  • Speed settling from 80 → 120 km/h within 10 s
  • Tube-based MPC maintained safe following gap ≥ 8 m under uncertainty
  • NMPC overtaking maneuver satisfied passenger-comfort constraints throughout

My Contribution

  • Analytical linearization of the car model around steady cruise conditions
  • Subsystem decomposition into independent lateral and longitudinal dynamics
  • Linear MPC controllers with terminal sets for both subsystems
  • Nonlinear MPC (NMPC) for complex maneuvers and overtaking with ellipsoidal constraints

Challenges & Solutions

The main challenge was maintaining feasibility of the QP across all deliverables, particularly when combining tube-based safety constraints with tight actuator bounds. Subsystem decomposition into independent lateral and longitudinal MPC controllers reduced problem size and improved solve times. Terminal set computation (MPT3 toolbox) was critical for guaranteeing recursive feasibility in the linear MPC stages.
