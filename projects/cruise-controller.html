Cruise Controller for a Car on Highway

Designed and implemented a Model Predictive Control (MPC) cruise controller for highway driving in MATLAB. The controller predicts future vehicle states over a receding horizon and optimizes throttle/brake commands to maintain target speed while respecting actuator constraints. Evaluated performance under varying road grades and lead-vehicle scenarios.

Key Results

  • Speed tracking error < 0.5 km/h in steady-state conditions
  • Smooth response without actuator saturation on 6° grades
  • Adaptive horizon tuning improved fuel efficiency proxy by 12%
  • Compared MPC vs. PID: MPC outperformed on constraint handling

Challenges & Solutions

Tuning the prediction horizon and weighting matrices was the main challenge. A short horizon resulted in aggressive control action; a long horizon increased solve time. An empirical sweep of horizon lengths (5–30 steps) identified 15 steps as the best trade-off. Constraint tightening ensured soft constraints on jerk did not cause infeasibility in the QP solver (quadprog).
