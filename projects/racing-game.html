Iterative Best Response of Zero-sum Racing Game

Modeled and simulated a head-to-head racing duel between two autonomous cars as a multi-stage zero-sum feedback game. At each time step, each car solves a quadratic program (cvxpy/OSQP) to anticipate and counter its opponent's move. The Iterative Best Response (IBR) algorithm alternates between the two players until saddle-point controls are reached. Vehicle kinematics follow a bicycle model in both Cartesian and Frenet coordinates, with collision avoidance enforced via elliptical safety constraints.

Key Results

  • Validated saddle-point behavior: both cars accelerate optimally on straight track
  • IBR convergence: relative cost gap |J₁ − J₂| ≈ 10% on average
  • Collision and track boundary constraints satisfied across all tested scenarios
  • Full pipeline implemented in Python with cvxpy/OSQP for real-time QP

My Contribution

  • Built the bicycle model for vehicle kinematics
  • Implemented coordinate transformation from Cartesian frame to Frenet frame
  • Formulated the racing interaction as a multi-stage feedback game where one player's gain is the other's loss

Challenges & Solutions

The main challenge was ensuring IBR converged to a saddle-point rather than cycling. Elliptical collision constraints introduced non-convexity, which was handled with slack-augmented QPs to maintain feasibility. Frenet-frame coordinate transforms were used to simplify track boundary constraints, decoupling longitudinal and lateral dynamics for cleaner QP formulation at each time step.
