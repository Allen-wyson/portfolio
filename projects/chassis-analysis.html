Chassis Structural & Vibration Analysis for Formula Student

Performed structural and vibration analysis for a Formula Student race car chassis at Delta Electronics. Built simplified FEA models in Abaqus to evaluate 20g impact survivability, identified potential failure points under crash loading, and conducted motor-induced vibration and resonance assessment to ensure the chassis avoided critical natural frequencies.

Quick Info

Duration: Jul–Aug 2023

Organisation: Delta Electronics

Role: CAE Engineer Intern

Key Results

  • Verified chassis survival under 20g frontal impact (safety factor > 2)
  • Identified 3 high-stress regions and recommended local reinforcement
  • Modal analysis showed first mode at 168 Hz — clear of motor excitation range
  • Simulation insights directly informed final design revisions

Challenges & Solutions

The chassis geometry was complex and simplifying it for FEA without losing structural fidelity required careful decisions about beam vs. shell element use. Tube sections were modeled as beam elements for global stiffness analysis, then shell elements were used locally at joints identified as high-stress regions. The motor excitation frequency range was used as the target exclusion zone in modal analysis.
