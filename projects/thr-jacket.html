THR Jacket — Temperature & Humidity Regulation

Group project for EPFL course ME-410: Mechanical Product Design and Development (Prof. Jamie Paik). Designed and fabricated a ski jacket that automatically regulates interior temperature and humidity based on sensor readings. Four cable-driven flap openings are actuated by servo motors. Contribution: PI control loop design, circuit design, and 3D modeling.

Challenges & Solutions

The primary challenge was designing a cable-routing system that allowed sufficient flap travel without straining the jacket fabric. Multiple guide tube placements were tested before settling on a routing that preserved waterproofing. On the control side, tuning the PI parameters required accounting for the slow thermal response time of the jacket interior, handled by adding a derivative filter to reduce oscillation.
