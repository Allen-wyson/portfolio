Soft Robotic Tongue for Speech

Designed and built a physical speech robot featuring a soft robotic tongue to study how tongue deformation affects vowel production. The system integrates pneumatic and tendon-driven actuation, interchangeable silicone lips, a servo-actuated jaw, and a silicone vocal fold excitation source. Conducted controlled articulation experiments and acoustic analysis (Praat) to evaluate formant behavior, highlighting challenges in reproducing textbook vowel acoustics in physical vocal tract models.

Challenges & Solutions

Reproducing human vowel acoustics mechanically is fundamentally difficult because the human vocal tract's geometry is shaped by continuous, fine-grained muscle control. The main challenge was achieving sufficient tongue deformation range while maintaining pneumatic seal integrity. This was addressed by iterating on silicone compound ratios and wall thicknesses. Acoustic output consistently differed from textbook vowel formants, which itself became a documented research finding about the limits of current soft robotic vocal tract models.
