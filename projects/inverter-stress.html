Stress Analysis on Traction Inverter

Conducted structural analysis of a commercial-vehicle power inverter to evaluate how assembly and manufacturing tolerances affect mechanical stress when the inverter is mounted onto the housing. Built FEA models in Abaqus, assessed deformation risks under misalignment scenarios, and provided design recommendations to ensure a robust, tolerance-resistant mounting configuration.

Quick Info

[ Inverter Assembly & Mounting ]
[ Stress Concentration Under Misalignment ]

Key Results

  • Quantified stress increase under 0.3 mm mounting misalignment
  • Identified two bolt locations as stress concentration sites
  • Recommended slot-hole modification reducing peak stress by 18%
  • Results validated against physical teardown observations

Challenges & Solutions

Modeling realistic tolerance scenarios required parametric Abaqus models where mounting positions could be varied systematically. A Python scripting approach (Abaqus scripting interface) was used to automate the batch-run of misalignment cases, reducing setup time from hours to minutes per case. Contact formulation between the inverter base and housing required careful penalty stiffness tuning to avoid numerical instability.
